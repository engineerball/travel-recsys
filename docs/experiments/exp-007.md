# Exp-007 — Bump steps_per_epoch 150→500 (2026-05-19)

## Changes from [Exp-006](exp-006.md)

| Change | Where | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| `steps_per_epoch: 150 → 500` | `configs/config.yaml` | 500 steps × 512 batch = 256k samples/epoch, covering ~86% of 292k train interactions (vs 26% before) |

**No model or data changes.**

**Motivation:** With 5x more training data (324k interactions), 150 steps/epoch only covered 26% of available data per epoch. Bumping to 500 ensures the model sees most of the dataset each epoch.

Side effect: warmup length grows from 450 → 1500 gradient steps (warmup_epochs=3 is epoch-based), changing the LR ramp shape.

## Notes

- **@50/@100 gains across the board** — more data per epoch improves recall at higher K; val loss confirms better overall representations.
- **Hit@10 regressed, especially article (−29%) and event (−16%)** — top-1 precision hurt despite better embeddings. Two likely causes:
  1. Longer warmup (1500 vs 450 gradient steps) changes LR ramp; model spends more time in low-LR regime before full-speed learning, favouring broad recall over tight ranking.
  2. More steps per epoch before each val checkpoint means the model is evaluated less frequently relative to gradient updates, reducing effective early-stopping resolution.
- **Val loss improvement does not guarantee Hit@10 improvement** — InfoNCE loss optimises embedding alignment globally; top-10 precision depends on fine-grained local structure that may not be captured by loss alone.

## Open issues

- `warmup_epochs` is epoch-based, so warmup length scales linearly with `steps_per_epoch`. Consider switching warmup to step-based (`warmup_steps`) to decouple from epoch size.
- Steps_per_epoch 500 helps @50/@100 but hurts @10. A middle ground (e.g. 300) may balance both; or keep 500 and accept the trade-off if retrieval is used at @50+.
- Article Hit@10 (0.020) is the lowest ever — may need dedicated investigation.
