# Exp-005 — Article behavior generator: theme + language bias (2026-05-19)

## Changes from [Exp-004](exp-004.md)

**No model changes.** Behavior data regenerated with smarter article sampling.

| Change | Where | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| `_THEME_ARTICLE_TYPE_BIAS` | `behavior_generator.py` | 15 travel_themes → additive article typeId weights, applied on top of persona prefs |
| `_LANGUAGE_COUNTRY_PREF` | `behavior_generator.py` | `home_country` → `(thai_w, non_thai_w)` multiplier; Thai users 3:1 prefer Thai content, US/UK 1:10 prefer English |
| `_parse_list` | `behavior_generator.py` | Extracted to module level (was nested inside `_user_persona_weights`) |

**Motivation:** Exp-004 noted article Hit@10 was capped by behavioral signal quality — persona simulator picked articles nearly randomly within article type, ignoring user language preference and travel_theme. Generated interactions were near-random for articles, so the model had nothing to learn from.

Files changed: `src/data/behavior_generator.py`, `data/generated/` (regenerated).

## Notes

- **Article +23% Hit@10, +50% Hit@100** — confirmed the Exp-004 diagnosis: behavioral signal was the ceiling, not model capacity. Theme + language biases make generated interactions meaningfully structured.
- **Event +69%/@50, +63%/@100** — unexpected large gain. Event sessions are now generated against users whose themes more coherently align with event categories, improving the training signal quality for event interactions too.
- **Accommodation −12% Hit@10** — soft regression, likely from distributional shift in training data (fewer accommodation-type sessions sampled as article/event weight grows). @100 slightly positive. Worth watching in Exp-006.

## Open issues

- Accommodation Hit@10 regressed. May need to investigate whether behavior distribution shift reduced accommodation training samples, or whether cross-type negative sampling now hurts accommodation more.
- Article Hit@10 still low (0.027 absolute). More themed article types in data or stronger affinity signal may help further.
- Event @10 slightly below Exp-001 baseline — stricter eval (6k vs 1.5k val) inflates difficulty; @50/@100 shows massive real gains.
